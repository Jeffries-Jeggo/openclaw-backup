# How to Cultivate Learner Agency

Learner agency refers to the capacity of students to take ownership of their learning, make informed choices, and actively participate in their educational journey.

## Four Ways to Build Learner Agency

### 1. Create an Environment Where Students Feel Supported
- Promote respect, empathy, and collaboration
- Ensure all students feel valued and supported
- Provide opportunities for self-reflection
- Acknowledge learner variability

**Did you know?** All learning occurs in the pre-frontal cortex. It is impossible to access this part of the brain when the brain stem and limbic system are under siege. Safety and belonging are critical to learning.

### 2. Provide Opportunities for Student Choice and Autonomy
- Allow students to select topics
- Choose their own projects
- Determine the format of presentations

Example: "If the firm goal is to write informational text, all students will write. Students can choose to handwrite, type, or use voice-to-text. They may have the option to work with peers or use tools like graphic organizers and sentence stems."

### 3. Encourage Critical Thinking and Problem-Solving
- Ask open-ended questions
- Encourage students to analyze information
- Provide opportunities for real-world problems
- Teach the power of micro-failure and productive struggle

### 4. Promote Metacognition and Self-Regulation
- Teach strategies for monitoring understanding
- Help students set goals
- Adjust learning approaches as needed

Prompt: "State your goal, describe your strategy, and reflect on whether the strategy is supporting your learning and how you know it is or isn't."

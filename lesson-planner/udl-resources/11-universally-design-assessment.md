# How to Universally Design Assessments

## Key Distinction: Marks vs. Grades
- **Mark/score**: Number or letter given to any student test or performance
- **Grade**: Symbol reported at the end of a period as a summary of student performance

## UDL Assessment Flowchart

### 1. Diagnostic and Formative Assessments
- Start with finding where students are with the content
- Use results to customize the lesson
- Provide important progress monitoring tool
- Help students self-differentiate

### 2. Assessments Aligned to Standards
- Based on standards and objectives
- Provide options for students to show what they learned
- Reduce "test anxiety"

### 3. Multiple Attempts to Resubmit
- Students learn from mistakes when given opportunities to correct them
- In standards-based grading, grades reflect current learning, not past mistakes

### 4. UDL-Inspired Rubrics
- Based on learning outcomes, not assessment method
- Provided to students with the assessment outline
- Support self-assessment and monitoring progress

### 5. Self-Report Marks
- Students reflect on their performance
- Effect size of 1.33 (anything over .4 is considerable)
- Great opportunity for mastery-oriented feedback

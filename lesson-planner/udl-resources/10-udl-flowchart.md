# UDL Flowchart: Moving Beyond Choice

Offering choice is just skimming the surface of full-scaled UDL implementation. When UDL is done well, its efforts result in learner agency: students who are purposeful and reflective, resourceful and authentic, strategic and action-oriented.

## Key Questions for Lesson Design

When designing a learning experience, ask:

1. **Engagement**: Have you addressed how you will design options for motivation? Ensure options for students to sustain persistence? Help students cope when things get difficult?

2. **Representation**: Have you offered a variety of materials and supports to assist students with comprehension?

3. **Action & Expression**: Have you allowed students to take ownership and choose how they express what they've learned?

4. **Goal**: Have your students met or exceeded that standard or goal?

UDL is a standards-based framework — if students don't meet a standard, you haven't done enough to remove barriers.

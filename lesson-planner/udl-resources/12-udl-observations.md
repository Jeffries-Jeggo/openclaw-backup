# 8 Focus Areas to Evaluate UDL in the Classroom

## 1. Learning Objectives
- Grade-level objectives visible to students
- Referred to throughout the lesson
- Grounded in standards with flexibility in how students achieve them

## 2. Classroom Culture & Belonging
- Environment affirms identity of all students
- Fosters sense of belonging
- Model inclusive language
- Invite students to contribute materials reflecting their experiences

## 3. Emotional Literacy
- Address social-emotional needs
- Embed self-regulation strategies (brain breaks, pacing choices, calming corners)
- Structured reflections on feelings and motivations

## 4. Collaboration
- Purposeful collaboration and collective learning
- Scaffolds: group norms, roles, sentence stems
- Peer feedback for academic and social connection

## 5. Self-Reflection
- Students regularly self-assess
- Graphic organizers, goal-setting prompts, digital trackers
- Connect strategies to objectives and identify next steps

## 6. Flexible Methods & Materials
- Varied resources: books, videos, manipulatives, digital tools
- Multiple pathways for inquiry (blended, project-based, small-group)
- Students mix and match resources and approaches

## 7. Flexible Assessments
- Not one-size-fits-all
- Blend formative checks (exit tickets, polls, peer reviews) with summative options
- Common rubrics ensure rigor
- Ongoing feedback helps students adjust strategies

## 8. Student Voice
- Students explain thinking, pose questions, suggest adjustments
- Feedback loops are fluid
- Learners help shape their own success
